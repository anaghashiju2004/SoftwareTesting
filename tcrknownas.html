THRISSUR FAMOUS FOR..

POORAM

Thrissur Pooram is a world-renowned Hindu temple festival held annually in Thrissur, the cultural capital of Kerala. Often referred to as the “Mother of All Poorams”, it is celebrated with unmatched grandeur, spirit, and traditional splendor. It is held at the Vadakkumnathan Temple, located in the heart of Thrissur city. The festival is not just a religious event—it is a massive cultural gathering that brings together people of all religions and backgrounds in a vibrant celebration of Kerala’s art, music, tradition, and community life.

GURUVAYOOR

Guruvayoor is a small but spiritually significant town located in the Thrissur district of Kerala, India. It is most famous for the Guruvayoor Sri Krishna Temple, one of the most sacred and visited pilgrimage sites in South India. Often referred to as the “Dwarka of the South”, Guruvayoor is known for its religious traditions, cultural richness, and spiritual atmosphere.

PUTHANPALLI

Puthanpalli, officially known as the Basilica of Our Lady of Dolours, is a grand Syro‑Malabar Catholic minor basilica in Thrissur city, Kerala. Built between 1929 and 1940 in soaring Indo‑Gothic style, it covers approximately 25,000 sq ft, making it one of the largest churches in India. The church is crowned by a 260‑foot (79 m) Bible Tower, the tallest in India and the third tallest in Asia, flanked by two front bell towers around 146 ft (45 m). Inside, the basilica features eleven altars, richly decorated interiors with murals, icons, and statues depicting scenes from the Scriptures. In 1992, Pope John Paul II bestowed upon it the status of a Minor Basilica, elevating its spiritual and historical significance.

THEKKINKADU MAIDANAM

Thekkinkadu Maidanam is a large open ground located in the center of Thrissur city, Kerala. It surrounds the famous Vadakkunnathan Temple, one of the oldest temples in South India. The word "Thekkinkadu" means "forest in the south" in Malayalam. This ground is a popular place for people to walk, relax, and gather. It is also the main venue for the world-famous Thrissur Pooram festival, which attracts thousands of visitors every year. Thekkinkadu Maidanam is considered the cultural heart of Thrissur and a symbol of the city’s rich traditions.

ATHIRAPALLY

Athirapally Waterfalls, located in Chalakudy Taluk, Thrissur District, is Kerala’s largest waterfall, cascading majestically around 81–82 ft (≈25 m) on the Chalakudy River, originating in the Western Ghats. Known widely as the “Niagara of South India”, the falls spread across about 330 ft (≈100 m) and pour through jungle-rich cliffs into multiple segmented plumes. The surrounding riparian forests harbor rich biodiversity—including hornbills (all four south Indian species in one region), lion‑tailed macaque, elephants, sambar, leopards, and endemic flora.

CHIMMINI DAM

Chimmini Dam, located in the Thrissur district of Kerala, is built across the Kurumali River, a tributary of the Karuvannur River. It is the largest dam in the district and is situated within the scenic Chimmini Wildlife Sanctuary, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and eco-tourists. Completed in 1996, the dam is surrounded by lush forested hills and offers a peaceful environment for visitors. Activities like coracle boating, birdwatching, and guided treks are available here. The area is rich in biodiversity and is home to various species of birds, butterflies, and small wildlife. With its calm waters and green surroundings, Chimmini Dam is an ideal getaway for relaxation and exploring Kerala’s natural beauty.

SAKTHAN THAMPURAN PALACE

Shakthan Thampuran Palace—formerly known as Vadakkechira Kovilakam—is a historic royal palace in Thrissur city, Kerala, commissioned by King Rama Varma “Shakthan” Thampuran around 1795 and later converted into a museum in 2005. Named after the visionary ruler famed for transforming Thrissur into a structured city, the palace blends Kerala’s traditional Nālukettu layout with Dutch colonial architectural elements such as high ceilings, thick walls, tall doors, and spacious corridors.

SAKTHAN THAMPURAN

Sakthan Thampuran (1751–1805) was a powerful ruler of the Kingdom of Cochin. He is known as the architect of modern Thrissur, as he developed the city by clearing forest land around Vadakkunnathan Temple and creating the famous Thekkinkadu Maidan. He also started the world-famous Thrissur Pooram festival in 1797. Sakthan Thampuran was a strong and just king who made many reforms and helped unite different communities in his kingdom.
